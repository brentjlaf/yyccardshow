Execution Plan

Master Checklist

Follow this in order and you’ll cover every essential step from business setup to post-show recap.

Foundation Vendors Marketing

Phase 1 — Foundation

Set the basics before you tell anyone.

Decide the Basics

  • Event name
  • Cards + collectibles scope
  • One-day show (recommended)
  • Target month (September)
  • Target size (30–40 tables)
  • Profit goal (ex: $3k+)

Business Setup

  • Sole proprietor is fine for first show
  • Separate bank account (recommended)
  • Register GST number
  • Payment methods: cash + Square/Stripe

Phase 2 — Venue & Legal

Lock the event with hard details.

Book the Venue

  • Contact 3–5 venues
  • Confirm tables/chairs included
  • Parking + washrooms
  • Load-in + tear-down timing
  • Sign rental agreement + pay deposit

Insurance & Compliance

  • Event liability insurance ($1–2M)
  • Venue named as additional insured
  • Confirm occupancy + signage rules

Phase 3 — Money Setup

Price it right and lock the rules.

Pricing Structure

  • Vendor table price + discounts
  • Admission price
  • Early entry price
  • Kids policy (free under 12)

Vendor Agreement

  • Table size + load-in rules
  • Refund policy
  • No counterfeit policy
  • No table sharing without approval
  • Behavior rules

Phase 4 — Vendors (The Engine)

Tables sold = show funded.

Vendor Outreach

  • Calgary card shops
  • Pokémon/TCG sellers
  • Sports card sellers
  • Online Instagram sellers
  • Facebook groups

Vendor Management

  • Track vendors in a spreadsheet
  • Collect name, email, tables, payment
  • Send confirmation + table numbers
  • Send load-in instructions (1 week prior)

Phase 5 — Sponsors

Optional but powerful for cash + legitimacy.

Plan Sponsorships

  • Decide sponsor tiers
  • Define placements (banners, posts, tables)
  • Create a 1-page sponsor sheet

Outreach Targets

  • Local card shops
  • Collectibles stores
  • Breakers / streamers
  • Supplies sellers
  • Ask for cash or prizes

Phase 6 — Advertising & Marketing

Cheap but effective promotion.

Core Marketing

  • Facebook Event page
  • Instagram content
  • Flyers (digital + print)
  • Google Maps event listing

Paid Ads

  • $10/day for 10–14 days
  • Target Calgary + card interests
  • Use vendor posts as creatives

Bonus Tactics

  • Giveaway post
  • Early-entry hype
  • Vendor spotlights

Phase 7 — Day-Of Operations

The show succeeds or fails here.

Timeline

  • 7–9am vendor load-in
  • 9–10am early entry
  • 10am–3pm general admission
  • 3–5pm tear-down

Must-Haves

  • Wristbands or stamps
  • Float cash ($300–$500)
  • Clear signage
  • Staff at door + roaming

Rule of Thumb

Do not sell cards yourself during show #1. Run the event.

Phase 8 — Post-Show

Lock in the next show while momentum is high.

Immediate Wrap

  • Thank vendors + sponsors
  • Post recap photos
  • Ask vendors about return tables

Plan the Next

  • Reserve your next date
  • Raise pricing slightly
  • Grow the email list
