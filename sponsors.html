Sponsor Strategy

How to Find Sponsors

Sponsors are where the show levels up. Focus on adjacent hobby businesses that already want your audience.

Who to Target Sponsorship Tiers Outreach Script

Who Actually Sponsors a Card Show?

Start with local hobby businesses. Skip big corporates for show #1.

Tier 1 (Easiest)

  • Local card shops
  • Pokémon / TCG stores
  • Sports memorabilia shops
  • Comic and toy stores
  • Breakers / streamers

Tier 2 (Very Doable)

  • Grading middlemen (PSA group submitters)
  • Supplies brands (binders, sleeves)
  • Local hobby stores (retro games, Lego)
  • Local resellers / buy-sell stores

Tier 3 (Skip for First Show)

  • Banks
  • Realtors
  • Big national brands
  • Slow approval cycles

Simple Sponsorship Tiers

Sell placements, not vague “exposure”. Keep it simple.

Supporting Sponsor

$250
  • Logo on website + flyers
  • 1 social post shoutout
  • Door signage

Event Sponsor

$500
  • Logo on website + flyers
  • Banner behind admission table
  • 1 vendor table included
  • 2–3 social posts

Title Sponsor

$1,000
  • “Presented by ___” naming
  • Largest logo placement
  • Prime vendor table
  • Mention in ads + announcements

What You’re Selling Sponsors

Specific placements that are easy to explain and deliver.

Sponsor Inventory

  • Website logo placement
  • Flyer / poster logo placement
  • Banner at admission table
  • Vendor table inclusion
  • Social media mentions

How Many You Need

  • 1 title sponsor
  • 1–2 supporting sponsors
  • That’s it for show #1

Outreach Script

Keep it short, direct, and non-cringe.

First Message

Hey! I’m organizing a Calgary Cards & Collectibles Show this September. We’re expecting ~30–40 vendors and a few hundred collectors. I’m reaching out to a few local shops to see if you’d be interested in being a sponsor or having a presence at the show. Would you be open to a quick chat or email?

Follow-Up

Awesome — thanks! Sponsorships range from $250–$1,000 and include logo placement, promo, and tables. Happy to send a quick one-pager with details.

One-Page Sponsor Sheet Must Include

No fluff. One page max.

  • What the event is (date, location, focus)
  • Who attends (collectors, families, flippers)
  • Why it’s good for them (direct buyers)
  • Sponsor tiers + pricing
  • Your contact info

Timing Tip

Reach out 8–10 weeks before the show after the venue and pricing are locked.

Extra Notes

Sponsors can provide value even when they won’t pay cash.

Non-Cash Options

  • Door prizes
  • Raffle items
  • Promo cards or packs
  • Discount codes

What Not to Do

  • Don’t oversell attendance
  • Don’t promise ROI you can’t prove
  • Don’t offer exclusivity in year one
  • Don’t chase sponsors before vendors
