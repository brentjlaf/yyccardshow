Everything You Need to Know About the Show

YYC Cards & Collectibles is a community-forward show built for collectors, hobby shops, and families. Expect a clean, well-run event focused on buying, selling, and trading.

Buy • Sell • Trade

Family Friendly

What the Show Is

A one-day collector experience focused on community, fair pricing, and quality inventory.

Collector-First Experience

Curated vendors, clear walkways, and a friendly environment for new and seasoned collectors.

Who it’s for: sports collectors, TCG players, slab buyers, families, and anyone who loves nostalgia collectibles.

Safe Buying & Selling

Meet real sellers, inspect inventory in person, and make confident trades.

Payment note: some vendors accept card, many prefer cash. Bring both if you can.

What’s Being Sold

A wide mix of cards, collectibles, and nostalgia pieces.

Sports Cards

Hockey, NBA, NFL, MLB, soccer, UFC, and more — singles, lots, and graded.

Expect: rookie hunting, set building, bargain boxes, and higher-end slabs.

Pokémon & TCG

Singles, sealed product, graded slabs, accessories, and binder finds.

Expect: packs, ETBs, playmats, sleeves, and trade-friendly inventory.

Collectibles

Toys, comics, nostalgia items, and related memorabilia.

Examples: Funko, retro figures, signed items, display pieces, and oddities.

Trade Zone Rules

Trading is welcome — keep it clean, respectful, and safe.

Where to Trade

Use the designated trade zone. Keep aisles clear and do not block vendor tables.

Trade at Your Own Risk

Inspect items carefully. The show does not verify authenticity on personal trades.

Respect Vendors

Do not use vendor tables as your trade station. If you’re negotiating, step aside.

Entertainment

Extra fun while you shop and trade. Entertainment stays on the perimeter to keep aisles flowing.

Raffles & Giveaways

Win sealed product, merch, and hobby accessories throughout the day.

Pro move: grab raffle tickets near the exit after you’ve done your buying.

Artist / Custom Sketches

Optional artist table for custom Pokémon-style sketches or collector art.

Great for: gifts, display pieces, and social content.

Family Corner

Optional face painting / small kid-friendly activity so parents can browse.

Note: kept away from the main aisles to avoid bottlenecks.

What to Bring

Small things that make your day smoother — and get you better deals.

Payment Options

Cash + Card. Many vendors accept card, but cash can unlock faster deals.

Tip: bring small bills for bargain bins.

Supplies

Bring sleeves/toploaders for big hits, and a small bag/box to protect your pickups.

Optional: a small flashlight for checking card surfaces.

Trade Prep

If you’re trading: price comps saved on your phone, and your cards organized.

Keep it quick: don’t hold up aisles while negotiating.

Safety, Security & Respect

Keep the room friendly, safe, and comfortable for everyone.

Be Cool

No harassment, no aggressive haggling, no scams. If staff asks you to stop, stop.

Kids: please keep children close in busy aisles.

Counterfeit Policy

Counterfeit items are not allowed on vendor tables. If something looks questionable, report it to staff.

Reminder: always inspect before you buy.

FAQ

Quick answers to the most common questions.

Can I bring kids?

Yes. Kids 12 and under are free with a paid adult admission.

Is re-entry allowed?

Yes. You can come and go throughout the day with a wristband.

Do vendors accept card payments?

Many vendors accept card and cash, but we recommend bringing both.

Are trades allowed?

Yes. Please use the trade zone for collector-to-collector trades and keep aisles clear.

Is there an ATM on-site?

Some community centres don’t have ATMs. Plan to bring cash if possible.

Can I bring a backpack or rolling case?

Yes. Just keep it tight to your body in aisles so you don’t clip tables or people.

Do you authenticate cards on-site?

Not at this time. Inspect items before purchase and buy from trusted vendors.

What items are not allowed?

Counterfeit items, stolen goods, and anything unrelated to cards/collectibles. Staff may remove prohibited items.

Is food available?

Some venues have concessions, some don’t. You may step out and return with your wristband.

Accessibility?

The venue has accessible entry and washrooms. If you need accommodations, contact us before the show day.
