Revenue First

Business Model & Profit Plan

A clear, repeatable model that makes money with tables + tickets while keeping costs controlled.

Pricing That Works Profit Example Legal & Admin

Revenue Streams

Use multiple streams so a slow door still leaves profit.

Primary Revenue

  • Vendor tables (core income)
  • General admission
  • Early / VIP entry

Secondary Revenue

  • Sponsorships (local shops, graders)
  • Raffles / giveaways
  • PSA/SGC/BGS group submissions
  • Food vendor cut / venue rev share

Profit Goal

  • Small show can clear $3k–$8k
  • Start simple and tight
  • Build repeatability

Right Size (Start Small)

Busy room > empty space. Aim for a legit but tight first show.

Ideal First Show

  • 25–40 vendor tables
  • 1-day event (Saturday)
  • 4–6 hours open to public

What You Want

  • Busy room
  • Tables sold out
  • Line at the door

What You Avoid

  • Empty space
  • Desperate table discounts
  • Overhead panic

Venue Options (Calgary)

Pick a venue that includes tables/chairs and allows proper load-in.

Best Venue Types

  • Community halls
  • Legion halls
  • Hotel conference rooms
  • Rec centre meeting rooms

Must-Haves

  • Tables + chairs included (critical)
  • Parking and washrooms
  • Power access
  • Insurance allowed
  • 2–3 hours load-in time

Typical Venue Cost

Plan for $800–$2,000 for a day. Avoid anything over $2,500 for show #1.

Pricing That Works

Keep pricing simple, fair, and profitable.

Vendor Tables

$80–$120
  • Sweet spot: $100 per table
  • 2 tables: $150
  • 3 tables: $210

Admissions

$5–$10
  • Kids under 12 free
  • Early entry: $15–$20
  • Average target: ~$8

Table Math

$4,000
  • 40 tables @ $100
  • Cashflow covered early
  • Use for venue + ads

Realistic Profit Example

A one-day show can still clear strong profit.

Revenue

  • Tables: $4,000
  • Admission: $1,600
  • Early entry (50 x $15): $750
  • Total: $6,350

Expenses

  • Venue: $1,500
  • Insurance: $150
  • Printing/wristbands: $150
  • Ads: $300
  • Buffer: $300
  • Total: ~$2,400

Profit

Estimated profit: ~$3,900 for one day.

Legal & Admin Basics

Keep it clean and simple — these are the non-negotiables.

Minimum Requirements

  • Event insurance ($1–2M liability)
  • Simple vendor agreement
  • Clear refund policy
  • Cash + Square/Stripe terminal
  • GST handling (register)

You Don’t Need

  • Full corporation (sole prop is fine)
  • Fancy contracts
  • Paid staff (volunteers work)

Filling Tables Fast

Vendors care about traffic, clear rules, and simple setup.

Who You Want

  • Sports card sellers
  • Pokémon / Yu-Gi-Oh sellers
  • Vintage + modern
  • Breakers (allowed)
  • Supplies sellers

Where to Find Them

  • Facebook groups
  • Local card shops
  • Instagram DMs
  • Previous show vendors

Vendor Priorities

  • Foot traffic
  • Buyers with cash
  • Clear setup rules
  • No last-minute chaos

Rules That Protect You

Put these on every vendor confirmation + website page.

  • No refunds after cutoff date
  • No table sharing unless approved
  • No counterfeit cards
  • No aisle blocking
  • No loud live breaks without permission

Why It Matters

Clear rules avoid drama and keep vendors wanting to return.

Scaling After Show #1

Once you succeed once, raise prices and build a repeat brand.

Raise Value

  • Increase tables to $120
  • Add a Sunday day
  • Add grading partner

Grow Revenue

  • Sponsor banner sales
  • Build a mailing list
  • Quarterly show cadence

Build the Brand

  • Repeatable format
  • Vendor trust
  • Reliable profits
